Design System · v1

Petro

The visual language of a satirical automated oil & gas marketer, housed in a moulded round-CRT exhibit machine. Clean, near-monochrome, deadpan. Everything here is reusable across new screens and products.

01

Colour

Not pure black & white, and never cream. A cool half-step off white for the screen, a soft slate ink instead of saturated black. The body of the machine is warm white; the bezel is near-black graphite.

02

Type

Two brand voices. Bricolage Grotesque (extrabold) carries every headline — tight, confident, slightly characterful. Calibre Light handles supporting copy for an airy feel. A system monospace, uppercase and bracketed, is reserved for machine cues.

03

The machine

Every screen is framed inside the same object: a bell-shaped white shell that domes over a graphite-ringed round CRT and flares to a planted base, with a suggested keyboard. The screen is the only lit surface.

Hi, I’m Petro.
Your automated oil & gas marketer
◀ ▶Browse ↵Select
  • Bell shell. A domed top that hugs the screen, pinching at the neck and flaring to a flat, planted base — moulded plastic, soft drop shadow, no hard corners.
  • Bezel + glass. A graphite ring with an inner shadow, holding a slightly vignetted off-white glass disc.
  • One screen size. Petro and the layout never resize between screens — the machine feels physical and fixed.
  • Keyboard. Suggested, never labelled. It signals "you drive this with keys," nothing more.
04

Layout rhythm

Every screen is the same three stacked zones, vertically centred with equal gaps. Content changes; the rhythm never does. This is what makes the flow feel like one continuous machine.

Zone top — a line
A headline (and optional subline). The machine speaking: a greeting, a question, a status.
Zone mid — Petro
The oil-can character, always the same size, gently bobbing. The constant anchor.
Zone bot — a cue
A control or feedback: key hints, a selector, a loading bar. Empty is fine.
05

Motion

Subtle, mechanical, never bouncy. Motion suggests a real CRT warming and a character that's quietly alive.

Power-on
A bright beam draws across, then blooms open to reveal the screen. Plays whenever the machine wakes.
Idle bob
Petro drifts up ~3.5px and back on a slow loop. He's alive, not animated.
Screen flash
A faint white wash on every screen change — a CRT refresh.
Fade-in
Content rises 4px into place over 0.4s. Used for screens and swapped lines.
Blob reset
An organic morphing ink shape that breathes between "done" and "start again."
06

Sound

A tiny live synth voice — no audio files. Filtered-noise clicks for touch, and short two-note-at-once chordal melodies for meaning: ascending is good news, descending means round again.

Browse tick
A soft high noise tick with a faint pitched blip.
Select lift
A click plus a quick rising major third — a small "yes."
Power-on C E G C
A warm ascending major arpeggio as the machine wakes.
Loading twinkle
A gentle pentatonic climb that tracks the progress bar.
Success fanfare
Ascending major thirds resolving into a bright held chord.
Round again cascade
A soft descending major chord cascade as it loops back.
07

Voice

Deadpan, satirical, matter-of-fact. The comedy comes from saying sinister things cheerfully. Sentence case everywhere; bracketed uppercase for machine status.

Headlines — chipper & human
"Hi, I'm Petro." · "Great, let's get started!" · "Now we're cooking!" · "Your idea is ready"
Prompts — quietly damning
"Who do you want to influence?" · "What emotion should we manipulate?"
Status cues — bracketed mono
[ Firing up the rig… ] · [ Profiling the demographic… ]
Thinking — absurd honesty
"Having deep strategic thoughts…" · "Cutting down some trees…"

Carrying this into new work

To build a new Petro surface (a product page, another screen) in a fresh chat or in Claude Code, bring these files as context — they hold every token and component above:

  • petro.css — all colour/type tokens, the bell shell, CRT, layout zones, motion keyframes.
  • fonts/ — the four Calibre weights (Bricolage loads from Google Fonts).
  • petro-device.jsx — the <PetroDevice> / <PetroCRT> shell components.
  • petro-flow.jsx — the Sound synth voice and the screen/state patterns.
  • assets/petro-oilcan-cut.png — Petro himself.

Rule of thumb: new screens keep the three-zone rhythm, one constant Petro size, ink-on-off-white only, and a bracketed mono cue where the machine "speaks."
